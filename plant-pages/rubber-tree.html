Rubber tree

Rubber trees, or Ficus elastica, are a part of the Moraceae family, and are native to the eastern parts of South and Southeast Asia. This large tree has been known to grow up to 10 feet tall indoors, with it's leaves growing up to a foot long or larger.

Light

Rubber trees need medium to bright, indirect light for 6 - 8 hours a day. If they don't get enough sunlight, their colorful leaves will begin to fade.

Water

It's best to water your rubber tree every 1 - 2 weeks, allowing the soil to dry out between waterings. During the summer you'll need to water more frequently.

Soil

Generic, well draining indoor potting mix will do your rubber tree just fine! Be careful to not get too dense of potting mix, as this may result in root rot.
